At the end of a suburban street, the Japanese parted and closed a front door on. Demian went back the way, I had stopped and awaited him in the middle of the street. With palpitations, I saw him to meet, upright and elastic, in a brown rubber coat, a thin stick hung on the arm. He came without his uniform Stepping up, close to me, took off his hat and showed me his old, bright face with the determined mouth and the peculiar brightness on the broad forehead.

"Demian!" I called.
